Object and Horizon Detection and Tracking on an Autonomous Boat

I worked on the Defense research project developing the Maritime Autonomy Surface Testbed (MAST) system. I was responsible for developing detection and tracking algorithms to inform the obstacle avoidance and route planning algorithms. Using a FLIR 360° camera, we were able to detect, localise and track objects at up to 100m range.

We also developed a horizon tracking algorithm which enabled estimation of the boat's roll and pitch. This was used to inform the range estimation of detected objects, using the camera calibration to cast a ray from the camera's location to the sea surface.
